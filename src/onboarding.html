⟳ ReactSync

Sync reaction videos with their source — frame-perfect, across any two tabs.

01

Open both videos in separate tabs

ReactSync works across any two tabs — YouTube, Twitch, Vimeo, local files, anything with a <video> element. Navigate each video to roughly the right position before syncing.

02

Select Tab A and Tab B

A Reaction video
→
B Source video

Tab A is the reaction video (the one with the reactor's commentary). Tab B is the original source video being reacted to.

03

Set the offset

The offset tells ReactSync how many seconds Tab A is ahead of Tab B.

Tab B (source)
0s 30s 60s
Tab A (reaction)
35s 65s 95s
offset = +35s  ·  Tab A starts 35 seconds into the source
  • Positive offset — Tab A is further ahead (most common: the reactor skipped an intro).
  • Negative offset — Tab B is ahead of Tab A.
  • Zero — both videos started at the same moment.
TIP Not sure what the offset is? Hit ◎ auto-detect offset in the popup — ReactSync captures a few seconds of audio from each tab and calculates it automatically. Works on most sites (not DRM-protected streams like Netflix).
04

Hit SYNC — then forget about it

From this point on, play, pause, and seek in either tab and the other follows automatically. A drift check runs every 60 seconds in the background to keep things tight.

AltShift↑ Nudge offset +0.5s
AltShift↓ Nudge offset −0.5s

These keyboard shortcuts work globally — you don't need to open the popup.
